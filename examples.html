Prompt Engineering Examples

Understanding prompt engineering becomes easier when we look at practical examples. By comparing different types of prompts, we can see how small changes in wording can significantly affect the quality of the AI response.

Example 1: Basic Prompt

Bad Prompt: “Tell me about AI.”

This prompt is very general and does not provide enough details. Because the instruction is vague, the AI may produce a broad or unstructured answer that might not match the user’s expectations.

Improved Prompt

Better Prompt: “Explain artificial intelligence in simple terms for beginners and provide two real-life examples.”

This improved prompt clearly specifies the audience (beginners) and asks for examples. Because the instructions are more detailed, the AI can generate a clearer and more structured response.

Example 2: Role-Based Prompt

Another effective prompt strategy is assigning a role to the AI. For example, a prompt might say:

“Act as a computer science teacher and explain the concept of machine learning to a student.”

By defining a role, the AI understands the tone and style expected in the answer. The response will likely be more educational and easy to understand.

Example 3: Step-by-Step Prompt

Some tasks require reasoning. In such cases, prompts can instruct the AI to explain the steps used to reach the final answer.

For example: “Explain step by step how a search engine works.”

This prompt encourages the AI to organize the explanation into clear stages, making the information easier to follow.

These examples show how prompt engineering can improve the interaction between humans and AI systems. With well-designed prompts, users can obtain accurate, structured, and useful information from AI models.
